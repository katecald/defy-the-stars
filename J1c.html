You stand on your balcony looking down at Romeo’s face, which is glowing in the moonlight. You ask him how he got into the garden and what he’s doing here. He responds in the manner you’re learning to expect from him: poetic words pour forth from his mouth as he elaborates on his deep and undying love that will endure any trials and hardships that come between you.

You try to keep your head straight. His words are intoxicating and you’re tempted to drink them in without a second thought. But then you remember that there are legitimate reasons that you shouldn’t be together, reasons you shouldn’t even be speaking right now. You voice these concerns: that this is all happening so fast, that it shouldn’t be happening, that you both should probably take some time to think this through, but every cautionary word you offer is countered with a lyrical declaration of his devotion to you.

Do you send him away and retreat into your room to have some time to process this?

Do you continue to talk with him on the balcony?
